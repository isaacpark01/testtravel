Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 13, 2026

Travia ("we", "us", or "our") operates the Travia website and travel planning application. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and protect your information when you use our service.

1. Information We Collect

We collect information you provide directly to us, including:

  • Account information: display name, email address, and hashed password when you register.
  • Trip data: itineraries, saved searches, pinned cities, and group board content you create.
  • Usage data: pages visited, features used, and interactions within the app, collected automatically.
  • Device information: browser type, operating system, IP address, and language preference.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect to:

  • Provide, maintain, and improve the Travia service.
  • Authenticate your account and protect against unauthorized access.
  • Sync your trips and boards across devices via Supabase.
  • Send transactional emails (account confirmation, password reset).
  • Understand how users interact with Travia to improve features.
  • Comply with legal obligations.

3. Data Storage and Security

Your data is stored in Supabase (PostgreSQL) hosted on AWS. We use row-level security (RLS) policies so each user can only access their own data. Passwords are never stored in plain text — only a bcrypt hash is retained. Data is encrypted in transit via TLS.

4. Cookies and Local Storage

We use browser localStorage to store your session token and language preference. We do not use third-party tracking cookies. If you click "Accept" on our cookie banner, we may set an analytics cookie in the future to understand aggregate usage patterns — no personal data is shared with advertising networks.

5. Third-Party Services

Travia integrates with the following third-party services:

  • Supabase — database and authentication (Privacy Policy)
  • Unsplash — destination photography (Privacy Policy)
  • Google Fonts — typography (Privacy Policy)

Flight, car rental, and hotel links redirect to third-party booking platforms. We do not control their data practices.

6. Your Rights (GDPR / CCPA)

If you are in the European Union, United Kingdom, or California, you have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you.
  • Request correction of inaccurate data.
  • Request deletion of your account and all associated data.
  • Object to or restrict certain processing.
  • Data portability — export your trip data in JSON format.

To exercise these rights, email us at privacy@Travia.app. We will respond within 30 days.

7. Data Retention

We retain your account data for as long as your account is active. Inactive accounts (no login for 24 months) may be deleted after 60 days' notice. You can delete your account at any time from your profile settings.

8. Children's Privacy

Travia is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe a child has provided us with personal information, contact us at privacy@Travia.app.

9. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify registered users by email when material changes occur. Continued use of Travia after the effective date constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.

10. Contact

Questions about this Privacy Policy? Contact us at privacy@Travia.app.
